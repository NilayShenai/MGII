Inventory Methodology

1. Dimensional Score Computation

The psychometric inventory evaluates participants along 8 independent, continuous psychological dimensions. For each dimension $D_k$ (where $k$ represents the dimension index), the raw score is calculated as a weighted sum of responses:

$$D_{\text{raw}}(k) = \sum_{i} \left( \text{Weight}_{k, i} \times \text{Answer}_i \right)$$

Where response inputs are mapped on a 5-point Likert scale: Strongly Disagree ($-1.0$), Disagree ($-0.5$), Neutral ($0.0$), Agree ($0.5$), and Strongly Agree ($1.0$).

2. Dynamic Bounds Calibration

To ensure exactly normalized output scales between 0% and 100%, the engine executes a dynamic bounds evaluation during initialization. It computes the theoretical maximum and minimum raw scores possible for each axis based on matching weights:

$$D_{\text{norm}}(k) = 100 \times \frac{D_{\text{raw}}(k) - \text{RawMin}_k}{\text{RawMax}_k - \text{RawMin}_k}$$

3. Salience-Weighted Vector Similarity

To match personal coordinates against the 72 centroid profiles, the engine calculates the weighted Euclidean distance across the 8-dimensional space. An adaptive Salience Weight vector $S$ is calculated dynamically to amplify structural dimensions:

$$\text{Distance}(U, G_j) = \sqrt{\sum_{k} S_k \times \left(U_{\text{norm}}(k) - \text{Centroid}_{j, k}\right)^2}$$

The final match percentage is computed using a dynamically adjusted scaling factor $S_{\text{scale}}$ to normalize distances across variable salience bounds:

$$\text{Match \%} = \max\left(0, 100 \times \left(1 - \frac{\text{Distance}(U, G_j)}{S_{\text{scale}}}\right)\right)$$

Where the scaling threshold $S_{\text{scale}}$ dynamically calibrates based on active salience weights:

$$S_{\text{scale}} = 120 \times \sqrt{\frac{\sum_{k} S_k}{8}}$$

Clinical Sources & Foundations

Foundational Multidimensional Models (BSRI / GIS)

Separation of masculine and feminine internalization into separate orthogonal scales is based on the Bem Sex-Role Inventory (BSRI) (Bem, S. L., 1974, Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology) and the Gender Identity Scale (GIS) (Kagan, J., 1964). These clinical models proved that masculine and feminine attributes exist independently rather than as opposite endpoints of a single bipolar spectrum.

Non-Binary & Multi-Axis Diagnostics (Tate / Joel)

Research validating multi-dimensional modeling for non-binary gender identities is drawn from Tate, C. C., et al. (2014, Journal of Sex Research, "Better Psychology for Sexual and Gender Diversities") and Joel, D., et al. (2015, PNAS, "Sex Beyond the Binary: A Mosaic of Brain, Mind, and Gender"). These papers establish that individuals exhibit unique, multi-axial mosaic configurations rather than simple linear classifications.

Somatic Congruence & Gender Care Standards (WPATH SOC-8)

The constructs behind Somatic Assigned Sex Congruence (GCI) and somatic distress (dysphoria indices) align with clinical metrics established in the Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People, Version 8 (SOC-8) published by the World Professional Association for Transgender Health (WPATH, 2022).

Temporal Dynamism & Fluidity Profiles (Diamond / Twist)

Theoretical work on temporal gender fluidity and intensity flux models follows Diamond, L. M. (2008, Female Sexual Fluidity, Harvard University Press) and Twist, J., & de Graaf, N. M. (2019, Clinical Child Psychology and Psychiatry, "Gender Diversity and Gender Dysphoria in Children and Adolescents"). These clinical papers outline criteria for temporal shifting, volatility scales, and partial gender configurations.

Neurodiversity & Xenic Conceptualization (Strang / Dewinter)

The intersection of autism/neurodivergent profiles with metaphorical gender experiences (Xenic Metaphors) is documented in Strang, J. F., et al. (2014, Archives of Sexual Behavior, "Increased Gender Variance in Autism Spectrum Disorders") and Dewinter, J., et al. (2017, Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders). These studies show a significant statistical overlap between atypical sensory conceptualizations and unique gender metaphors.
